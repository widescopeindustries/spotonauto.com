## Launch Description (for Product Hunt body)

AI models are starving for structured, domain-specific data. Most automotive content online is HTML soup — ads, affiliate links, navigation, and generic cross-vehicle fluff.

**AllOEMManuals AI Training Feed** serves factory service manual data for 300,000+ vehicles as clean markdown.

No HTML to parse. No affiliate links. No ads. Just structured, vehicle-specific OEM repair data:

- Torque specs, fluid capacities, belt routing
- DTC codes and diagnostic flowcharts
- Wiring diagrams and component locations
- Step-by-step repair procedures
- Knowledge graphs linking components, codes, and systems

Built for:
- LLM training and fine-tuning
- RAG pipelines
- Automotive AI agents
- Repair assistants and copilots

**Pricing:** $0.01 per page via x402 micropayments. Volume discounts kick in at 100K and 1M pages/month.

We also gate AI training crawlers that scrape without attribution — search engines and citation bots that send traffic get free access.

## Maker Comment (first comment)

Hey Product Hunt,

I’m Lyndon — Army veteran, former auto shop owner, and definitely not a software developer.

I started AllOEMManuals because I spent years flipping trucks and fixing cars, and I kept seeing the same problem: the factory service manual data exists, but it’s buried in PDFs, paywalled sites, and noisy HTML pages that are terrible for machines to read.

Then AI blew up. And I realized the same data I was organizing for DIYers was exactly what LLMs needed — but they needed it clean.

So we built the AI Training Feed:
- One corpus. Two formats.
- Humans get the browseable site with affiliate links for parts.
- AI companies get clean markdown with no noise.
- Both pay their fair share — humans with attention/clicks, crawlers with x402 per page.

We’re already seeing 100K+ bot requests per day from Meta, Anthropic, and Apple crawlers. Now they can either pay or stop scraping our human site for free.

Happy to answer questions about x402, automotive data, or how a 31F Signal Soldier ends up running a data API.

— Lyndon
