# LinkedIn Post — Veteran Story

March 23, 2003.

That was the day I went over the berm into Iraq. I was 20 years old, a 31F Network Switching Systems Operator attached to an MP unit. I turned 21 in Baghdad.

A year later I was back in Germany, carrying something I didn't have a name for yet.

I started drinking to blackout. Got in a lot of trouble. The Army demoted me to E-1 and gave me a General Under Honorable Conditions discharge.

Nobody screened me for PTSD.
Nobody told me I might still qualify for VA benefits.

So for twenty years, I believed the VA wasn't for me.

I was wrong.

In 2022, a YouTube video from a veteran named Combat Craig landed in my feed. He had the same discharge I did — and he said veterans like us can still qualify for VA disability compensation.

I filed an Intent to File in 2023.

One condition. Three appeals. Six months later: 100% Permanent and Total.

That rating didn't just mean a monthly check. It meant stability. For the first time in twenty years, I could build something without the ground shifting under me.

I lost my auto shop to COVID. But I taught myself to build websites with AI tools. I founded AllOEMManuals.com — factory repair data for 300,000+ vehicles. I registered Widescope Industries LLC as a Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business. I bought a house with a VA home loan.

I'm not a software developer. I'm a combat tech vet who grew up turning wrenches.

But I learned that discipline plus stability plus not quitting is enough.

If you have a General discharge and you've been told the VA isn't for you — hear me: you are almost certainly wrong.

File an Intent to File today. It costs nothing. It protects your effective date. Just start.

Don't give the VA twenty years like I did.

---

*Lyndon Bedford is an Iraq War veteran and founder of AllOEMManuals.com and Widescope Industries LLC, a Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business.*
